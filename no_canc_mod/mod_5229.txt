HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:  This is a 19 year-old woman who complained of five days of increasing sore throat, which has progressed to the point where she is unable to swallow food denies any shortness of breath.
MEDICATIONS:  She is currently on no prescription  ALLERGIES:  She has no known drug allergies.
PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:  This was an alert woman who was uncomfortable secondary to throat pain frequently spitting saliva into a cup.
Examination of the oropharynx revealed edema of the left tonsillar pillar displacing the uvula to the right and there was also edema of the left soft palate and exudate in the left tonsillar area.
Examination using a fiberoptic laryngoscope revealed supraglottic edema, which obscured the view of the vocal cords.
Abdomen was soft and nontender.
There is no peripheral rash or edema.
A CT scan of the neck was performed, which showed extensive soft tissue swelling as well as a small abscess in the left peritonsillar area extending into the hypopharynx.
By the next day she had significant improvement, but still complained of being unable to swallow.
She had no fevers greater then 100 degrees subsequently and on repeat examination her swelling had decreased.
See OMR note for further details.
DISCHARGE DIAGNOSIS: Left peritonsillar cellulitis and abscess.
