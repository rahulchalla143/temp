The patient presented to the Emergency Room, in which a head computer tomography revealed a small left frontal subarachnoid hemorrhage.
Neurosurgery was consulted; however, the patient was also noted to have a right cortical evulsion fracture of the dorsal triquetrum.
Right hand with third finger swollen and tenderness throughout, and an ulcer at the snuff box.
RADIOLOGY-IMAGING:  A head computed tomography as above.
Pelvis and left tibia no fractures.
A computer tomography of the spine showed degenerative disease, but no acute fracture.
A computer tomography of the face revealed no fractures.
A plain film of the right knee revealed no fractures.
A repeat computer tomography the next day showed the subarachnoid hemorrhage to be stable.
