HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:  This is a 55-year-old female with non-Hodgkin lymphoma, status post chemotherapy treatment with fludarabine one month ago who presents with sore throat and throat swelling since this morning.
No fever or chills.
No shortness of breath.
No rhinorrhea.
She was found to have supraglottic watery edema including epiglottic fold and false cords.
A 1-cm area was noted with adenoid hypertrophy.
ALLERGIES:  No known drug allergies.
This is a pleasant female in no acute distress.
Extremities had no swelling or tenderness.
HOSPITAL COURSE:  This is a 55-year-old woman with recurrent non-Hodgkin lymphoma presenting with airway edema, but no stridor compromise.
ENT was consulted and agreed with steroid course.
Oncology believed that the patient had no significant nodal disease in the neck, and therefore cancerous cause of the throat swelling was unlikely, although still a possibility.
The most likely cause was either an acute viral or bacterial pharyngitis or epiglottitis and agreed with steroid and antibiotic use and continuous oxygen monitoring.
No lung disease.
DISCHARGE DIAGNOSES:  Supraglottic edema and stenosis.
