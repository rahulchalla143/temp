HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:   This is a 55 year old female with a history of Barrett's polyps, gastroesophageal reflux disease, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and diabetes mellitus who had a follow-up esophagogastroduodenoscopy and colonoscopy the day prior to admission, and presented to the Emergency Department with bright red blood per rectum times four, and right sided abdominal pain.
In the Emergency Department, on arrival, her heart rate was 68, blood pressure 138/69, and she had an episode of bright red blood per rectum.
The patient then became lightheaded and had a systemic blood pressure of 74/36.
The patient denied fever or chills, shortness of breath, chest pain, palpitations, nausea or vomiting, constipation or diarrhea, urinary symptoms.
History of colonic polyps.
Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.
Diabetes mellitus.
Total abdominal hysterectomy secondary to excessive bleeding.
Father died of myocardial infarction at the age of 71; mother with polyps which were benign.
No history of colon cancer, ulcerative colitis or Crohn's.
Cardiac examination revealed a regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs or rubs.
Abdominal examination showed a soft nondistended, tender to palpation on right side greater than epigastric without rebound or guarding.
