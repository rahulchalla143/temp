HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:  This is a 20 year-old male with no significant past medical history who was in his usual state of health until eight days prior to admission when he noticed some dysuria and frequency.
He also had noted enlarging groin lymph nodes, which he thought was related to a urinary tract infection.
He had denied fevers or sweats.
He had gone to his school's health center where he also had some neck lumps.
He subsequently noticed that a black eye developed over his right eye after the bowel movement and then a blind spot developed about "5 degrees off to the right" in his right eye.
He was also placed on Ceftazidime for a fever to 101.8 and neutropenia.
He was then transferred to [**Hospital1 69**] for emergent leukophoresis and management of acute leukemia.
ALLERGIES:  No known drug allergies.
There is no history of intravenous drug abuse.
His HEENT examination is significant for periorbital ecchymosis over the right orbit with scleral hemorrhage evident in the right eye.
His neck showed large approximately 8 cm nodes in the anterior cervical region as well as submental cervical nodes.
His cardiovascular examination was regular rate and rhythm with no murmurs, rubs or gallops.
His groin revealed some inguinal adenopathy bilaterally as well as right pressure dressing was intact with a Quinton catheter had been for phoresis.
Extremities showed no pedal edema and warm and well perfuse extremities without rash.
His alkaline phosphatase was elevated at 231.
Acute leukemia:  Because of the high white count of 430,000, the patient was transferred to [**Hospital1 1444**] for acute phoresis for management of leukostasis.
The patient stated he felt very anxious due to some tightness in his chest.
This demonstrated acute myelogenous leukemia of M4 type.
He was put on Cefepime instead of Ceftazidime for initial fever and neutropenia.
There was no evidence of tumor lysis during his hospital course in the Intensive Care Unit.
He did have acute renal failure, which was thought due to lysis on kidney, which could be a complication of his acute leukemia.
He had mild evidence for disseminated intravascular coagulation, but he had no bleeding from any sites on his body.
An initial blood culture, which was showing a gram stain positive for gram positive coxae was retracted due to negative confirmatory test in the microbiology laboratory.
He has had no further febrile episodes.
Acute renal failure:  Again as stated the patient presented with a creatinine of 1.8, which is felt to be due to lysis on kidney.
His renal failure resolved with aggressive hydration and was normal by the time he was transferred to the Four [**Hospital Ward Name 1950**] Bone Marrow Transplant Unit.
Bleeding:  The patient did have some episodes of bleeding with the placement of the right internal jugular catheter as well as a right groin Quinton catheter for phoresis.
He experienced no bleeding complications from the removal of the Quinton catheter.
