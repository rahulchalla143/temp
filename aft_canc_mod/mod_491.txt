HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS AND HOSPITAL COURSE:  The patient is a delightful 58-year-old woman, who presented with bilateral pneumonia and was found to have an obstructive tumor of the carina.
After at least six week period of pulmonary rehabilitation, she was rescanned, and found to have no evidence of metastasis.
We took her to the operating room, and performed a mediastinoscopy to mobilize the pretracheal plane, and found no evidence of mediastinal adenopathy in the paratracheal region.
There is also no evidence of invasion of the pulmonary artery.
