On the day of delivery, contractions were noted during routine testing and the decision was made to deliver.
The infant was placed on continuous positive airway pressure and transferred to the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit.
CARDIOVASCULAR ISSUES:  The infant's blood pressures have remained normal during this admission with no requirement for volume or blood pressure medications.
Her length was 45.75 cm (75th to 90th percentile).
Her head circumference was 34 cm (25th to 50th percentile).
GASTROINTESTINAL ISSUES:  Single phototherapy was started on [**10-31**] for a bilirubin level of 8.6.
Phototherapy was discontinued on [**11-2**] with bilirubin level of 6.8 and a rebound bilirubin level was decreased at 6.
Direct antibody test was negative.
The infant has not had a subsequent hematocrit checks since the initial hematocrit of 48.
INFECTIOUS DISEASE ISSUES:  Although there were no sepsis risk factors other than prematurity, given the infant's initial respiratory distress, a 48-hour sepsis evaluation was initiated.
Her chest x-ray showed only low lung volumes and haziness which were consistent with respiratory distress syndrome.
Initial complete blood count revealed a white blood cell count of 16.3 (with 62% polys, 28% lymphocytes, and no bands), her hematocrit was 48, and her platelets were 130.
Blood cultures were sent, and the infant's was treated with ampicillin and gentamicin until the blood cultures remained negative for 48 hours.
Blood cultures results were subsequently negative.
NEUROLOGIC ISSUES:  Given the infant's gestational age, a head ultrasound was not required.
An ophthalmology examination was not required.
Feeds on discharge were Premature Enfamil 26 calories per ounce at 150 cc/kg per day almost entirely by mouth.
Car seat testing has not been performed.
State newborn screen was sent on day of life three with reported normal results, and a repeat state screen was sent on [**11-10**].
