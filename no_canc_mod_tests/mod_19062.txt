HOSPITAL COURSE:  At presentation, a head CT revealed a coronal fracture, skull base fracture, left parietal subdural hematoma, and possible epidural hematoma with some shift in effacement of the ventricle along with a left intraparenchymal hemorrhage and a subarachnoid hemorrhage.
The Neurosurgery Service continued to follow him throughout his stay in the intensive care unit, and he was taken for a diagnostic angiogram on postoperative day number 19.
He did have a swallowing study evaluation prior to advancing his diet, and he did well with both solid foods and thin liquids.
