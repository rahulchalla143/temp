Mother presented for a cesarean section delivery due to breech presentation.
Mother received spinal anesthesia.
A large amount of oral secretions requiring repeat bulb suctioning.
Positive pressure for approximately two to three minutes.
Infant with spontaneous respirations but with retractions and flaring.
RESPIRATORY ISSUES:  The infant initially required blow-by oxygen and received one liter of nasal cannula at 100% for increased respiratory distress.
The infant was transitioned from nasal cannula to room air by day of life two.
FLUIDS/ELECTROLYTES/NUTRITION ISSUES:  The infant was initially nothing by mouth receiving 60 cc/kg per day of D-10-W.  Enteral feedings were started on day of life one, and the infant has been on full feedings of Enfamil 20 calories per ounce with breast milk 20 calories per ounce ad lib by mouth q.4h.
GASTROINTESTINAL ISSUES:  The patient did not receive phototherapy during this hospitalization.
The infant did not receive any blood transfusions during this hospitalization.
The infant 48 hours of ampicillin and gentamicin, and the blood cultures remained negative to date.
Hepatitis B vaccine was recommended prior to discharge.
