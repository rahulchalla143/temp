Patient had positive loss of consciousness and right shoulder pain and numbness in her hands.
She received a right axillary brachial bypass with greater saphenous vein.
The patient received two units of packed red blood cells.
During her hospital course, the patient was also repleted multiple times with potassium, magnesium and phosphorus.
On day three of her hospital course, the patient had a decreased hematocrit and received a transfusion of two units of packed red blood cells.
The patient continued to recover well from her surgery and was evaluated by Physical Therapy and Occupational Therapy over a period of two to three days.
She had increased mobility and received therapy instruction on activities of daily living and mobility.
A plan was developed with the Case Manager and Occupational Therapy and Physical Therapy for home Occupational Therapy, as well as continued therapy on part of the patient with help of family members.
