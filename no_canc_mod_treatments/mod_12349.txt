Mother was admitted to the hopsital approximately three weeks prior to delivery with concerns of course of betamethasone [**5-27**].
Delivery occurred via cesarean section on [**6-18**] secondary to concerns regarding fetal well-being.
Genitalia, anus, hips and back were normal.
The patient was intubated and received a dose of surfactant.
The patient was subsequently taken off the CPAP and weaned to room air by day five of life and has remained stable on room air since that time.
The patient was not treated with caffeine and has not had significant issues with apnea of prematurity.
Cardiovascular:  The patient did exhibit mild to moderate hypotension and required blood pressure support with dopamine up to 6 to 10 mcg per kilo per minute over the first 40 to 72 hours of life.
A murmur was heard at approximately day two to three; an echocardiogram was performed on [**6-21**], day of life three, which revealed a structurally normal heart with the exception of a small ventricular septal defect; no PDA was present and function was normal bilaterally.
The patient was able to be weaned off of Dopamine by day four of life and subsequently remained hemodynamically stable throughout the remainder of hospitalization.
Fluid, electrolytes and nutrition:  The patient initially maintained on parenteral nutrition and was gradually begun on enteral feeds and day five or six of life.
These were advanced without difficulty and the patient was gradually transitioned from gavage feedings to oral feedings.
The infant was able to take adequate volumes entirely by [**7-2**] and by the time of discharge the patient had been taking all feeds by mouth with adequate volumes and adequate weight gain for several days.
The patient has maintained urine and stool output.
Gastrointestinal:  The patient did exhibit mild to moderate hyperbilirubinemia of prematurity requiring photo therapy for approximately five days.
The patient has been treated with iron supplementation.
Infectious disease:  The patient did receive Ampicillin and Gentamycin for the first 48 hours of life with negative blood cultures and benign clinical course.
Routine health care maintenance:  Hepatitis B vaccine was given on [**6-29**].
