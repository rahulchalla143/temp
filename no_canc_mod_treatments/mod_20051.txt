Delivery was a planned repeat Cesarean section.
He was given blow-by oxygen and stimulation but developed grunting, flaring and retracting shortly after birth.
He was transferred, then, to the newborn Intensive Care Unit after visiting briefly with his parents.
HOSPITAL COURSE:  Respiratory:  Initially, baby was maintained in room air but developed an oxygen requirement of nasal cannula up to 50 cc of 100% flow.
He remained on nasal cannula through day of life two, with breathing pattern comfortable tachypnea, at 60's to 80's.
He was noted to have hypoglycemia upon admission with a dextrose stick of 35, for which he received a bolus 2 ml/kg.
D-sticks ranged from 49 to 96 on intravenous fluids.
Enteral feeds were initiated on day of life one with breast feeding with Similac 20 p.o.
Initial feeds were taken slowly but improved as his respiratory status improved.
Intravenous fluids were weaned off by day of life 3.
On [**7-12**], at 9 a.m., the IV was heparin locked with dextrose sticks of 79 and 75 after intravenous fluids were discontinued.
There were no blood products given during this admission.
Infectious disease:  Ampicillin and Gentamycin was initiated after a blood culture was obtained upon admission.
Blood culture remained negative at 48 hours and Ampicillin and Gentamycin were discontinued.
