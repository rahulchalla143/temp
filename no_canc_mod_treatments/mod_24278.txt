The infant was delivered by repeat cesarean section with Apgars of 9 and 9.
SUMMARY OF HOSPITAL COURSE BY SYSTEMS: RESPIRATORY: Due to her initial symptoms of respiratory distress she was placed in low flow nasal cannula oxygen at 25 cc per minute.
She did continue to have apnea and bradycardia which resolved with passage of time, but evaluation included cardiac and CNS etiologies as described below.
Enteral feedings were introduced on day of life #1 and she was advanced to full volume feedings with her IV fluids discontinued by day of life #4.
She remained on full enteral feedings with an ad lib volume exceeding her minimum of 80 ml per kilo per day throughout her hospitalization.
She required no blood transfusions during this hospitalization.
Because her symptoms resolved so rapidly, she was never started on any antibiotics and had no further concerns regarding sepsis for the duration of her hospitalization.
