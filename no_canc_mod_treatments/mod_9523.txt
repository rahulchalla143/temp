The patient was admitted to [**Hospital3 3834**] for monitoring and was started on a nitroglycerin drip.
Cardiac catheterization was performed on that day, which showed LMCA 80% distal stenosis, left anterior descending coronary artery 80% proximal, 90% mid, 80% distal involving D2 stenosis, LCX subtotally occluded, right coronary artery 80% proximal, 80% distal, 80% proximal posterior descending coronary artery and 60% mid posterior descending coronary artery occluded.
The patient was brought to the Operating Room on [**2108-11-2**] and a four vessel coronary artery bypass graft was performed.
The left internal mammary coronary artery was brought to the left anterior, reverse saphenous vein was brought to the diagonal, reverse saphenous vein graft was brought to the obtuse marginal and reverse saphenous vein graft was brought to the posterior descending coronary artery.
The patient received medical management postoperatively two doses and was subsequently transferred to the Cardiothoracic Intensive Care Unit unit where she did very well and was able to be transferred to the floor on postoperative day two where she continued to be diuresed and to do quite well in terms of her oxygenation.
