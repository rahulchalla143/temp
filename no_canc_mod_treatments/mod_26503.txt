Pregnancy was complicated by cervical shortening and preterm labor resulting in admission and treatment with betamethasone at 27 weeks and then recurrent at 31 weeks leading eventually to cesarean section delivery due to progressive labor.
The baby was treated with caffeine from day 6 through day     11 of life.
FLUIDS, ELECTROLYTES AND NUTRITION: Infant was initially     begun on IV fluids with enteral feeding introduced on day     of 2 and advanced without difficulties.
By the time of     discharge the infant is feeding Enfamil 24 or breast milk     24 calories per ounce made with Enfamil Powder on an ad     lib basis, taking 140 to 150 cc per kilo per day.
Infant was on iron therapy, which was increased     from 2 mg/kg/day to 4 mg/kg/day.
IMMUNIZATIONS GIVEN: Hepatitis B vaccine was given on     [**12-30**].
IMMUNIZATIONS RECOMMENDED: Synagis immunization is     recommended for (1) all infants born at less than 32 weeks,     2) born between 32 and 35 weeks with 2 of the following: Day     care during RSV season, a smoker in the household,     neuromuscular disease, airway abnormalities, or school     age siblings; or 3) with chronic lung disease.
Influenza immunization is recommended annually in the     fall for all infants once they reach 6 months of age.
