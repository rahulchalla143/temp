LABOR AND DELIVERY HISTORY:  Infant was a brow presentation with evidence of late decelerations on fetal monitoriong necessitating a stat cesarean section.
The infant received positive pressure ventilation and eventual intubation; 5 and 10 minute Apgars were 5 and 7 respectively.
RESPIRATORY:  Initial impression was respiratory distress in the delivery room necessitating intubation as part of the resuscitative effort.
The infant was given boluses of normal saline and bicarbonate to correct metabolic acidosis.
This corrected with aggressive fluid resuscitation.
FLUIDS, ELECTROLYTES, AND NUTRITION:  Infant was kept NPO in the immediate postnatal period, but then slowly allowed to feed.
The infant received 48 hours of antibiotics and cultures have been negative.
Antibiotics were started empirically simply for poor perfusion after delivery.
Immunizations received:  The infant has not yet received hepatitis B immunizations.
