After the vaginal delivery of twin number one this twin developed bradycardia, which was responsive to maternal position, but the infant became less tolerant of labor, leading to the decision to deliver by cesarean section.
She received one dose of surfactant and extubated to room air on day of life number one where she has remained.
Cardiovascular status:  She received some volume resuscitation after admission to the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit and then required pressor support for the first 24 hours of life.
Enteral feeds were begun on day of life number two and advanced without difficulty to full volume feedings by day of life number six.
At the time of discharge she is taking 24 calorie breast milk made with Enfamil powder or 24 calorie Enfamil on an ad lib schedule.
Gastrointestinal status:  She was treated with phototherapy for hyperbilirubinemia of prematurity on day of life number three until day of life number six.
She has received no blood product transfusion during this Neonatal Intensive Care Unit stay.
She is receiving supplemental iron to give her 2 mg per kilogram per day of iron.
Infectious disease status:  She was started on Ampicillin and Gentamicin at the time of admission for sepsis risk factors.
The antibiotics were discontinued after 48 hours when the blood cultures were negative and the infant was clinically well.
She had triple antibiotic ointment initially to the lacerations on her labia.
The infant has received no immunizations at this time.
Immunizations recommended, Synagis RSV prophylaxis should be considered from [**Month (only) 359**] through [**Month (only) 547**] for infants who meet any of the following three criteria, born at less then 32 weeks, born between 32 and 35 weeks with plans for day care during the RSV season, with a smoker in the household, or with preschool siblings or with chronic lung disease.
Influenza immunizations should be considered annually in the fall for preterm infants with chronic lung disease once they reach six months of age.
Before this age the family and other care givers should be considered for immunization against influenza to protect the infant.
