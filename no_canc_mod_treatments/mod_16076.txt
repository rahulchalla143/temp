HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:  The patient is a 25 year old man with a history of asthma requiring inhaled steroids, bronchodilators and marked by greater than two episodes of intubation for airway protection.
He presented to the Emergency Department with the acute onset of shortness of breath, wheeze and rash two days after being seen in the Emergency Department for similar complaints.
He was treated with Benadryl, steroids, Pepcid and given epinephrine and discharged home.
In the Emergency Department, the patient received subcutaneous epinephrine, intravenous Benadryl 50 mg, Solu-Medrol 125 mg intravenously, Famotidine 20 mg intravenously, Atrovent and Albuterol nebs as well as inhaled racemic epinephrine and Heliox.
He was also briefly placed on an epinephrine continuous infusion of 3 mcg per minute and then transferred to the Intensive Care Unit for further evaluation.
MEDICATIONS: Flovent 220 mcg four puffs twice a day.
Singulair 10.
Ranitidine 10.
Albuterol one to two puffs twice a day.
He received standing Diphenhydramine as well as steroids, inhaled bronchodilators and leukotriene antagonist.
Of note, the patient had two episodes of evanescent urticaria which responded well to supplemental diphenhydramine.
The patient was seen by the allergy service which recommended several tests, which are pending at the time of this dictation and include a tryptase level, a serum immunoglobulin E (IGE) level and a RAST panel.
Heliox was discontinued and after brief administration of supplemental oxygen by nasal cannula, he was able to breathe on room air comfortably and maintained adequate oxygen saturation greater than 97%.
