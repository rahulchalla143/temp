Maternal blood type A negative, antibody negative, group B strep status unknown, hepatitis B surface antigen negative, and rapid plasma reagin nonreactive mother.
The mother received spinal anesthesia with artificial rupture of membranes at delivery by cesarean section with initial Apgar scores were 7 at one minute of age and 8 at five minutes of age.
There was initial moderate respiratory distress, and continuous positive airway pressure was initiated.
There, the respiratory distress continued and the infant was intubated and received two doses of surfactant.
She was weaned from her ventilatory support and extubated by day of life three (on [**10-30**]) to continuous positive airway pressure support initially at 6 cm of water and then to 7 cm of water.
The infant was weaned to nasal cannula and progressed to room air by day of life five.
Subsequently, the infant has been comfortable in room air with minimal retractions and has not required any additional respiratory support.
The infant was started on parenteral nutrition, but enteral feeds were initiated on day of life three and advanced without problems.
At the time of transfer, the infant was tolerating full enteral feeds of 150 cc/kg per day of Premature Enfamil 26 calories per ounce, taking almost all of the feeds by mouth.
Phototherapy was discontinued on [**11-2**] with bilirubin level of 6.8 and a rebound bilirubin level was decreased at 6.
The infant has not required any transfusions.
Blood cultures were sent, and the infant's was treated with ampicillin and gentamicin until the blood cultures remained negative for 48 hours.
SENSORY ISSUES:  Audiology testing with a hearing screen with automated auditory brain stem responses on [**11-10**] with normal results.
No immunizations have been given.
