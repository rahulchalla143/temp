This pregnancy was uncomplicated until the mother presented on the day of delivery in spontaneous labor.
The infant was born via spontaneous vaginal delivery emerging vigorously with Apgars of 8 and 8 at 1 and 5 minutes.
He had molding on his head and fontanels were soft and flat.
SUMMARY OF HOSPITAL COURSE BY SYSTEMS:  Respiratory: He presented initially with grunting, flaring, and retracting, and was placed in nasal cannula O2.
He remained in nasal cannula O2 until day of life 4 when he then weaned to room air where he has been stable on room air since that time.
He has had no issues with apnea or bradycardia and has not required any methylxanthine therapy.
Fluid, electrolytes, and nutrition: He was NPO on admission and IV fluids were started at that time.
Enteral feedings were started on day of life 2.
He reached full enteral feedings and came off IV fluid by day of life 4.
He is presently taking Enfamil 20 with iron or breast milk ad-lib with a minimum of 100 mL per kilogram per day and is feeding very well.
Ampicillin and gentamicin were given for 48 hours and discontinued subsequently when the blood culture remained negative.
