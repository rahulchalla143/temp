The decision was made to bring to cesarean section because of decreased growth.
Infant vigorous in the Delivery Room with Apgar's of 8 at one minute and 8 at five minutes; given blow-by oxygen and stimulation.
The infant was brought to the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit for management of prematurity.
At that time, the infant was started on caffeine citrate which did not show improvement and apnea was profound enough to require intubation.
The infant has not had any more apnea or bradycardia since day of life three, so caffeine citrate was discontinued on [**11-28**].
FLUIDS, ELECTROLYTES AND NUTRITION:  Enteral feedings were started at 20 cc per kg per day on day of life one and due to growth restriction, the infant was also started on total parenteral nutrition and enterolipid on day of life one.
The infant started feeding advancement of 10 cc per kg twice a day on day of life two and had normal dextra sticks until day of life three, when the infant was noted to have apnea and bradycardia.
At that time, the infant was advancing on total volume and was currently on 120 cc per kg per day total fluids and at that time was on 60 cc per kg per day of premature Enfamil 20 calories per ounce or breast milk 20 calories per ounce.
The dextrose stick on day of life three was ten and the infant required a total of seven D10 boluses of 2 cc per kilo and also required advancement to 140 cc per kilogram per day and was also made n.p.o.
At that time, the infant also required a UVC placement to infuse the D20.
The UVC was discontinued and a PICC line was placed to infuse the D20.
The hypoglycemia resolved by day of life four and the intravenous fluids were decreased and feedings were resumed on day of life four and started at 20 cc per kilogram per day and continued to advance 15 cc per kilogram twice a day.
The glucose and the intravenous fluids were decreased with normal glucoses to 12.5.
The PICC line was removed day of life six.
The infant continued feeding advancement without difficulty and reached full volume feedings by day of life eight and is currently at 150 cc per kilogram per day of breast milk 20 calories per ounce or premature Enfamil 20 calories per ounce.
Glucoses have remained stable during the feeding advancement and have been 70 to 133.
GASTROINTESTINAL:  The infant did not receive phototherapy this hospitalization.
The infant did not receive any blood transfusions this hospitalization.
INFECTIOUS DISEASE:  The infant received 48 hours of ampicillin and gentamicin.
Ampicillin and gentamicin were discontinued on day of life two.
On day of life three, with increased apnea and bradycardia, a CBC, differential and repeat blood culture were drawn and Vancomycin and gentamicin were started.
The infant recovered rapidly and the antibiotics were discontinued at 48 hours.
Immunizations recommended:  Hepatitis B prior to discharge.
The infant has not received any immunizations this hospitalization.
