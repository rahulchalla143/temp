This was a repeat cesarean section for preterm labor.
At two hours of age, the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit team was called to evaluate the infant for grunting and he was admitted to the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit.
PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:  Admission physical examination reveals a vigorous, non dysmorphic infant; anterior fontanel soft, open and flat.
HOSPITAL COURSE: Respiratory status:  The infant required nasopharyngeal continuous positive airway pressure for about the first 48 hours of life when he was weaned to nasal cannula and then to room air on day of life three, where he has remained.
Enteral feeds were begun on day of life number one and advanced without difficulty to full volume feeding.
He is breast feeding and also feeding formula 20 calories per ounce, on an ad lib schedule.
Gastrointestinal status:  He was treated with phototherapy from day of life number three until day of life number five.
Phototherapy was discontinued on day of life five with a bilirubin of 12.1 total and direct of 0.4.
He has never received any blood product transfusions.
Infectious disease:  He was started on ampicillin and gentamicin at the time of admission for sepsis risk factors.
Antibiotics were discontinued after 48 hours, when the blood cultures were negative and the infant was clinically well.
The infant is discharged on a combination of formula and breast feeding on an ad lib schedule.
He received his first hepatitis B vaccine on [**2198-8-14**].
