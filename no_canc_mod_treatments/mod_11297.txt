Patient was intubated at the scene and was found unresponsive.
Patient was subsequently admitted to the Surgical Intensive Care Unit where a chest tube was placed on the right.
His hospital course was complicated by nephrogenic diabetes insipidus with a tremendous amount of urine output requiring a large boluses up to 12 liters of free water and D5W to maintain normal serum sodium.
The serum sodium went up as high as in the 170s, however, with aggressive fluid replacement, we were able to bring his sodium down to the 140s.
He also had a ventricular drain placed by Neurosurgery which persistently showed that he had increased intracranial pressure anywhere up 14 up to as high as 30s requiring intermittent boluses of mannitol.
