Cardiac catheterization showed Type A aortic dissection and Cardiothoracic Surgery was consulted.
The patient was seen in the Catheterization Laboratory and was brought emergently to the Operating Room where he underwent repair of a Type A aortic dissection with replacement of the ascending aorta with #26 shawl weave graft, and resuspension of the aortic valve.
The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred from the Operating Room to the Cardiothoracic Intensive Care Unit.
He was weaned from all vasoactive intravenous infusions.
His sedation was discontinued.
He was weaned from the ventilator and successfully extubated.
For that he was pancultured, also on postoperative day #2, the patient's chest tubes were removed and he was transferred to the floor for continuing postoperative care and cardiac rehabilitation.
