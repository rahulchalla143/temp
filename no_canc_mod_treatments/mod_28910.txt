Mother presented on day of delivery for routine follow up and was found to be severely hypertensive with proteinuria.
Subsequent evaluation revealed mildly elevated liver function tests and mildly decreased platelets consistent with HELLP syndrome prompting cesarean section delivery.
Mother was treated with hydralazine and magnesium prior to delivery and received one dose of penicillin 3 hours prior to delivery.
She was started on enteral feedings on day of life 1.
She has not required any intervention.
The infant has not required any blood transfusions.
