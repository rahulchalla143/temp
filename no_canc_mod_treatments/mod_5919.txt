In the Emergency Department he was tachycardiac and hypoxic but not hypotensive.
He was intubated given his hypoxia refractory to six liters nasal cannula or nonrebreather.
He was started on phenylephrine for hypotension then transferred to the Fennard Intensive Care Unit.
He was aggressively treated with pressors, broad spectrum antibiotics, and Acyclovir for shingles.
He was maintained on mechanical ventilation.
On the third hospital day at the results of a family meeting, the decision was made to withdraw care and make the patient "comfort measures only," at which point he was maintained on Fentanyl drip only.
