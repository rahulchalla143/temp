HOSPITAL COURSE:  The patient was admitted and made NPO and had an nasogastric tube placed.
He was started on Ampicillin, Ceftriaxone and Flagyl.
Several days later he required a second drain to be placed to drain his pelvic collection.
On [**2158-7-18**] the patient underwent an uncomplicated sigmoid colectomy with primary anastomosis.
One of the drains had perforated the small bowel in two separate portions of small bowel.
Small bowel resections were performed at these sites.
His postoperative course was notable for several episodes of hypotension requiring normal saline fluid boluses and a short stay in the Surgery Intensive Care Unit.
He was treated with Zosyn and Ciprofloxacin.
He was kept on bowel rest and was restarted on total parenteral nutrition.
On postop day six the patient's nasogastric tube, Foley, JP drain and telemetry were all discontinued.
The patient was transfused 2 units of packed red blood cells for a hematocrit of 23.3, which had been steadily declining over the proceeding several days.
His PCA was discontinued and he was initiated on oral pain medication on postop day ten.
His antibiotics were discontinued on postop day six.
MEDICATIONS ON DISCHARGE:  Percocet one to two po q 4 to 6 hours prn pain, iron sulfate 325 mg po q day, Lopressor 25 mg po b.i.d.
