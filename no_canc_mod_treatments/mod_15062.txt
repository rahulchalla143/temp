The patient was started on Amoxicillin when follow-up labs indicated increased LFTs.
A stent was therefore placed.
Following this stent placement, the patient developed increased nausea and abdominal discomfort localized in the epigastric region with subsequent development of mild jaundice.
MEDICATIONS ON ADMISSION:  Vitamin B, Folic Acid.
CT of the abdomen performed at the time of admission showed positive stranding around the pancreas with stent in place.
After placing a Swan, the patient was aggressively fluid resuscitated with repletion of electrolytes.
Two units of packed red blood cells were administered, and Zosyn was empirically initiated.
Cultures continued to be negative, and PICC line was inserted to provide nutritional support.
As a result, Metoprolol was initiated with Mysoline.
At the time of discharge, the patient was tolerating a regular diet without any abdominal discomfort, and white blood cell count was normalized with amylase and lipase decreasing.
DISCHARGE DIAGNOSIS:  Status post ERCP, acute pancreatitis.
DISCHARGE MEDICATIONS:  Protonix 40 mg p.o.
b.i.d., Haldol 0.5 mg p.o.
t.i.d., Lopressor 37.5 mg p.o.
b.i.d., Mysoline 75 mg q.5 days.
